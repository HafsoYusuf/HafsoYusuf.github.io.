YOU CAN HELP END STUDENT LOAN DEBT

You might be wondering how you can help solve an issue that almost seems indestructible and just a way of life. Below are two suggestions for how you can make a difference.

Learn More

Here is a list of resources you can use to learn more about this issue.


  • Student Debt Crisis Center (SDCC). A people-powered nonprofit that advocates for Americans with student loan debt and work with legislators to end the student debt crisis.
  • The Education Trust. A national nonprofit that works to close opportunity gaps that affect Black and Latino students and low-income students. They are equity-driven and acknowledge that systemic racism and student loan debt are connected.
  • Student Debt Strike - Debt Collective. A national debtors' union that demands the full cancellation of federal and private student loans They are on a student loan strike where they refuse to pay a cent of their loans. Their movement isn't only about student loan debt, and they have abolished more than 5 billion dollars in debt.

Call Your Representatives and Senators

In July 2021, the House introduced H.R.4797 - Student Loan Relief Act. The goal of this bill is to discharge outstanding and certain federal student loan debt; it outlines the method of loan discharge, excludes the loan discharges from taxable income, and it requires the Department of Education to automatically place each federal student loan borrower in administrative forbearance for at least a one-year period.


While H.R.4797 - Student Loan Relief Act has been introduced by the House, but it has been stonewalled by opposing parties. We need your help for this bill to be passed by both the House and Senate and then on to the President's office so it can be signed into law.
